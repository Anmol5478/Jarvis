Delhi, officially the National Capital Territory of Delhi (NCT), is a sprawling metropolis in northern India. It's a city of contrasts, steeped in history yet constantly evolving.  Here are a few interesting tidbits:

* **Ancient and Modern:** Delhi has been continuously inhabited for over 2,500 years. It's been the capital of numerous empires, leaving behind a rich tapestry of historical monuments like the Red Fort, Qutub Minar, and Humayun's Tomb, alongside modern architecture and bustling markets.

* **Two Cities in One:**  Delhi is essentially two cities: Old Delhi, characterized by narrow lanes, bustling bazaars, and Mughal-era architecture, and New Delhi, a planned city with wide avenues, government buildings, and colonial-era architecture.

* **A Melting Pot of Cultures:** People from all over India and beyond call Delhi home, creating a vibrant mix of cultures, languages, and cuisines. This diversity is reflected in the city's festivals, food, and art.

* **Street Food Paradise:** Delhi is renowned for its delicious and affordable street food. From spicy chaat to buttery parathas and flavorful kebabs, there's something to tantalize every taste bud.

* **Green Spaces:** Despite its urban sprawl, Delhi has several parks and gardens offering respite from the city's hustle and bustle.  The Lodi Garden, for example, houses tombs from the 15th and 16th centuries within a tranquil green setting.

* **Political Hub:** As the capital of India, Delhi is the center of the country's political activity, housing the Parliament, the President's Estate, and numerous government ministries.
